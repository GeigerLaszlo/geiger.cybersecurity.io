My goal was creating a honeypot system available on Windows, and it needs to act as a real server with open ports. It was exposed on the internet with a fake login site and reported every attempt. If the threat actors use the CEO's email address (who assigned me to this task), it must be sent to the CEO immediately by email and SMS.

I have installed a SPLUNK agent connected to our SPLUNK SIEM and wrote rules for various cases. It was a really interesting project; I enjoyed it and learned about honeypots and alerts.

MITTRE ATT&CK Heatmaps & Gap analysis

I have collected threats in specific sectors and analyzed them to get a comprehensive picture of their effectiveness and popularity. Based on these analyses, I have scored the threat actor groups and merged them into a heatmap. I have used the MITRE ATT&CK framework for this purpose. I received an asset inventory from our client and began determining the coverage of the MITRE ATT&CK heatmap by analyzing the asset features and capabilities. After creating the second heatmap (which represents the coverage of the MITRE ATT&CK framework based on the client's infrastructure), I subtracted the company's heatmap from the sector's heatmap. This provided a picture of the real coverage of the sector based on the MITRE ATT&CK framework.

Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)

I have developed a concept for creating weekly or monthly security incident handling reports for clients. This involves dealing with queries in Splunk SIEM, where agents installed on client computers are connected with the Manager module. The summarized findings are compiled into comprehensive reports.

Additionally, I ensure that these reports include actionable insights and recommendations to address any identified vulnerabilities or incidents. Regular reviews of the data help in refining security strategies and improving overall incident response. This proactive approach not only keeps clients informed but also enhances their security posture over time.
